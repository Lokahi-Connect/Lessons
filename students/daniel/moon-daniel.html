What is the Moon?
The Moon is a satellite — an object in space that orbits something else. The Moon is Earth’s only natural satellite.
🔭
Word noticing — satellite
The word satellite comes from Latin satelles, meaning attendant or companion. The Moon is Earth’s companion in space.

Stop 1 — The Surface

The Moon has dark flat areas, bright rocky areas, and many craters. We will notice one feature at a time.

Focus for this page

Noticemaria · highlands · craters
RememberDark does not mean water.
Word studyhigh + land → highland
Read this pageShort chunks with replay support

A NASA view of the Moon’s surface from above.

Visual map of the surface

This drawing labels the three main surface features.

⬛
Maria — dark flat plains
Maria are large, dark, flat basins on the Moon. The word comes from the Latin word for seas, but there is no water there.
⛰️
Highlands — bright rocky areas
Highlands are bright, rough, rocky areas. They are older than the maria.
💥
Craters — bowl-shaped holes
Craters are made when rocks from space strike the Moon’s surface.

Regolith — the Moon’s surface dust

Apollo 11 bootprint in lunar regolith.

Quick memory helper

  • Regolith = dust + broken rock
  • No wind
  • No rain
  • Footprints can stay for a very long time
👣
Why the footprint stays
The Moon is covered with regolith, a layer of fine dust and broken rock. There is no wind or rain on the Moon, so the footprint is still there.
🔬
Word noticing — highland
highland is a compound word built from two free bases: high + land → highland.

Stop 2 — Gravity & Mass

The Moon has less mass than Earth, so its gravity is weaker. That changes how people move.

Focus for this page

Key wordmass = how much matter
Big ideaLess mass → less gravity
RememberMoon gravity is about 6× weaker

🌕
Moon

📏
What is mass?
Mass is how much matter an object contains. The Moon has less mass than Earth, so it has less gravity.
6️⃣
A six-times difference
If you weigh 60 pounds on Earth, you would weigh about 10 pounds on the Moon.

Stop 3 — Moon Phases

The Moon does not change shape. We simply see different amounts of its sunlit side.

Focus for this page

Key wordreflect = bounce light back
Big ideaPhases come from light
RememberWaxing = growing · waning = shrinking
Read this pageUse the phase cards one at a time

The eight Moon phases in one cycle.

Why phases happen

As the Moon orbits Earth, we see different amounts of the lit side.

☀️
The Moon reflects sunlight
The Moon does not make its own light. It reflects light from the Sun.
🔄
One full cycle
The Moon’s phase cycle repeats in about 29 days, which is close to one month.
