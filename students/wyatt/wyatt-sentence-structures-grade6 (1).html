Why This Matters in MTG

In the beginning, there was the Word. And the Word had structure.

— Jace Beleren, in the Tome of the Sphinx

You already know that Magic cards have types. Instant. Sorcery. Creature. Enchantment. Planeswalker. The type tells you what the card does and the rules that govern it.

Sentences work the same way. Every sentence has a structure type — and that structure is built from smaller units called clauses, the same way a card's abilities are built from keywords and text.

There are four sentence structure types. By the end of this, you'll be able to identify all four on sight — and understand exactly why each one is built the way it is.

◈ ⚡
SIMPLE
Instant // One Clause
One independent clause. Fast. Direct. Can't be countered by complexity.
◈◈ ⚔️
COMPOUND
Sorcery // Two Equal Clauses
Two independent clauses joined by FANBOYS. Equal power on both sides.
◈◈ 🌀
COMPLEX
Enchantment // Dependent Bond
One independent + one dependent clause. A subordinating conjunction creates the link.
◈◈◈ 🐉
COMPOUND-COMPLEX
Legendary // Full Enchantment
Two or more independent clauses plus at least one dependent clause. Maximum power.

SECTION 2 OF 6

The Two Clause Types — Your Core Creatures

Everything builds from two clause types. Think of them as your two core creature types — you need to know what each one does before you can play the game.

🛡️ INDEPENDENT CLAUSE

Has a SUBJECT + PREDICATE and can stand on its own as a complete thought. It doesn't need anything else to make sense.

"Teferi altered the timestream."  ✔ Complete.
"Chandra unleashed the flame."  ✔ Complete.
"Garruk hunted across the planes."  ✔ Complete.
🔗 DEPENDENT CLAUSE

Also has a subject and predicate — but it cannot stand alone. It starts with a SUBORDINATOR that makes it depend on another clause to be complete.

"Because Teferi altered the timestream..."  ⚠ Incomplete. Waiting for more.
"Although Chandra was powerful..."  ⚠ Incomplete.
"When Garruk hunted across the planes..."  ⚠ Incomplete.
⚡ COORDINATING CONJUNCTIONS
Join two independent clauses as equals.

for and nor but or yet so

Mnemonic: FANBOYS
🌀 SUBORDINATING CONJUNCTIONS
Begin a dependent clause — creates time, cause, condition, or contrast relationships.

because although when if since while unless after

The formulas for the four types:

⚡ SIMPLE = IC
⚔️ COMPOUND = IC + FANBOYS + IC
🌀 COMPLEX = IC + DC
🐉 COMPOUND-COMPLEX = IC + FANBOYS + IC + DC

🔬 A Word Worth Noticing

You've been using the word independent a lot. It turns out that word is built — not invented. Look at its structure:

depend → meaning: to rely on something else
in- + depend +ent → independent
The prefix in- means "not." So independent literally means "not depending on anything else" — which is exactly what an independent clause does. It doesn't rely on another clause to be complete.

Same logic: a dependent clause literally depends on another clause. The spelling tells you the meaning.
