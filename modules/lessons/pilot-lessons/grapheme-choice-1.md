# Lesson Title
Why Do We Choose One Grapheme Instead of Another?

## Big Idea
Spelling represents meaning and structure. Grapheme choice is often guided by word relationships, not just sound.

## Why It Matters (Teacher)
Students often believe spelling is random or sound-based only. This lesson builds the understanding that spelling choices are influenced by meaning, word families, and consistent orthographic patterns.

## Student-Friendly Aim
Sometimes one sound can be spelled in different ways. Let’s figure out why.

## Prior Knowledge Needed
- Awareness that words carry meaning
- Basic understanding of sounds in words
- Exposure to related words (e.g., sign, signal)

---

## Explicit Instruction

Say:

English spelling is not random.  
When we spell a word, we are often showing how that word is connected to other words.

Sometimes a grapheme is chosen to keep that connection visible, even if the pronunciation changes.

We are going to look for meaning connections to help explain spelling choices.

---

## Key Terms
- base
- related words
- grapheme
- spelling choice

---

## Guided Inquiry

### Step 1: Compare words

Show:
sign  
signal  

Ask:
- What do you notice about these words?
- Do they seem connected in meaning?
- What stayed the same in spelling?

### Step 2: Add more

sign  
signal  
signature  

Ask:
- What part stays the same across all three?
- Does the pronunciation stay the same?

### Step 3: Notice

Guide students to:
- The <sign> base stays consistent
- The pronunciation shifts, but spelling holds meaning

---

## Worked Examples

### Example 1
sign → signal  

Explain:
The <g> helps show the connection to signal, even though we don’t hear it in sign.

---

### Example 2
heal → health  

Explain:
The vowel sound changes, but the spelling helps show the words are related.

---

### Example 3
act → action  

Explain:
The base <act> keeps its meaning connection even as the word grows.

---

## Practice

### Supported Practice

Group these words:

heal  
health  
healthy  

Ask:
- What stays the same?
- What changes?
- Why might the spelling stay similar?

---

### Independent Practice

Provide:

sign  
signal  
signature  

act  
action  
active  

Ask students to:
- Circle the part that stays the same
- Explain how the words are connected

---

### Transfer Task

Give a new set:

cave  
cavity  

Ask:
- What is the base?
- Why might the spelling stay connected?

---

## Grapheme Choice Focus

Ask explicitly:

Why do we keep the spelling <sign> even when we don’t hear the <g>?

Guide toward:
- It helps show meaning connections
- It links related words
- Spelling is not only about sound

Then extend:

Could we spell “sign” without the <g>?

Discuss:
- It would break the connection to signal and signature

---

## Reflection

Ask:
- What did you notice about spelling?
- Did spelling always match sound?
- What helped you understand the spelling choice?

## Supplemental Supports

### Teacher Notes
- Emphasize meaning before sound
- Do not overcorrect pronunciation shifts
- Focus on noticing patterns, not memorizing rules

### Extension
- Find another word family with a “silent letter”
- Build a word matrix (teacher-guided)

---

## Done Criteria

- Students identify a shared base
- Students recognize meaning connections
- Students understand spelling may not match pronunciation
- Students can explain one grapheme choice using meaning
- Cognitive load remains manageable
