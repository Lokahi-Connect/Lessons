Big Idea
What Is a Decimal?
📖 Read This First
A decimal is a way of showing parts of a whole number.

The decimal point is the dot ( . ) in a number like 0.3 or 1.75.

Everything to the left of the dot is a whole number.
Everything to the right of the dot is a part of a whole.
2 . 4 7

hundredths
In 2.47: the 4 is in the tenths place, and the 7 is in the hundredths place.

Place Value
Tenths
📖 Read This First
Tenths happen when you split something into 10 equal parts.

If you have 3 out of 10 pieces, that is 0.3 — three tenths.

The tenths digit is the first number after the decimal point.

Place Value
Hundredths
📖 Read This First
Hundredths happen when you split something into 100 equal parts.

If you have 27 out of 100 pieces, that is 0.27 — twenty-seven hundredths.

The hundredths digit is the second number after the decimal point.
🖱️ Click squares — each one is one hundredth (0.01)
0 out of 100 = 0.00

Number Line
📖 Read This First
A number line shows where numbers live between 0 and 1.

0.5 is exactly in the middle — halfway between 0 and 1.
0.25 is one quarter of the way from 0 to 1.
