🧱 Stop 1 of 6 — Word Investigation Wall

What's hiding inside these words?

These words appear in today's passage. Before you read, let's investigate what they mean — using the parts inside them as clues. Tap each word to reveal its meaning and structure.

established
🔍 Look at the base: <establish>
Meaning: set up or started officially

Word sum:
establish + ed → established

The suffix <-ed> tells us this happened in the past.
abandoned
🔍 Look at the base: <abandon>
Meaning: left completely; no longer used or lived in

Word sum:
abandon + ed → abandoned

The suffix <-ed> tells us this happened in the past.
occupied
🔍 Look at the base: <occupy>
Meaning: lived in or used by people

Word sum:
occupy + ed → occupied

Notice: <y> changed to <i> at the join — that's a convention called a Change.
discovered
🔍 Look at the parts: <dis-> + <cover> + <-ed>
Meaning: found something that was not known before

Word sum:
dis + cover + ed → discovered

The prefix <dis-> means "apart" or "away." To dis-cover is to uncover — to take the cover away from something hidden.
attracted
🔍 Look at the parts: <at-> + <tract> + <-ed>
Meaning: drew people toward something

Word sum:
at + tract + ed → attracted

The bound base <tract> means "pull." To attract is to pull toward. You can also see it in: tractor, distract, contract.
expository
🔍 Look at the base: <expose> / <posit>
Meaning: writing that exposes (makes visible) information

Word sum:
ex + posit + ory → expository

The prefix <ex-> means "out." The bound base <posit> means "put" or "place." Expository writing puts ideas out into the open.
🧐 Stop 2 of 6 — Fact vs. Opinion

Fact or Opinion?

Before we read, you need one important tool: being able to tell a fact from an opinion.

✅ FACT
  • Can be proven true
  • Based on research or measurement
  • Uses numbers, names, dates

"Lima is the capital of Peru."

💬 OPINION
  • Is someone's personal view
  • Cannot be proven true or false
  • Often uses: best, worst, beautiful, scary

"Pizza is the best food."

Life in Ashcroft, Colorado 🏔️

📅 Founded: 1880
⛏️ Started as: Silver mining camp
👻 Now: Ghost town
🏚️ Buildings left: 9 total
☀️ Summer visitors: ~75 per day
Base morpheme Suffix Prefix

I went to Ashcroft for a class trip. It was amazing. When I arrived, I thought, "Wow! This place looks scary and quiet." My teacher, Mrs. Matthews, smiled and said, "That's because Ashcroft is a ghost town."

The class gasped. "Are there really ghosts here?" asked my friend Taylor. Mrs. Matthews laughed and told us that a ghost town is actually just an abandoned city or town that was once occupied by people.

Mrs. Matthews explained to the class that in 1880, Ashcroft was established as a silver mining camp. Silver was used in photography and medicine. "Sadly, the town's success didn't last long. People left Ashcroft after discovering new silver in Aspen. People were attracted to bigger towns," said Mrs. Matthews.

As the sun set across the sky, the class imagined life in Ashcroft. We danced, laughed, and ran into the fields. It was wonderful.

📚 Stop 4 of 6 — Two Kinds of Text

Literature vs. Informational Text

The Ashcroft passage mixed both kinds of writing. Can you tell which parts were informational and which were literature?

📖 Literature Text

  • Imaginary characters
  • Read for pleasure
  • Can include opinions & feelings
  • Stories, novels, plays

🔗 Both

  • Use language conventions
  • Have settings
  • Can tell a story

🗞️ Informational Text

  • Real people & events
  • Tells facts about the world
  • No personal opinions
  • Expository writing

✍️ Stop 5 of 6 — Hamburger Writing

Build a Strong Paragraph 🍔

Every strong expository paragraph is built like a hamburger — a strong top, juicy middle, and a solid bottom. You investigated the words. You read the passage. Now let's write.

🍞 Introduction — Top Bun

Your main idea — what is this paragraph about? This sentence tells the reader what to expect.

🥬 Supporting Detail 1

First piece of evidence or information from the text

🍅 Supporting Detail 2

Second piece of evidence or example

🥩 Supporting Detail 3

Third piece of evidence or example

🍞 Conclusion — Bottom Bun

Wrap it up — restate your main idea in a new way.
