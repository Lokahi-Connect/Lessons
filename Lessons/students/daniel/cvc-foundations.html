The FLSZ Convention

After a single vowel grapheme at the end of a single-morpheme content base, the letters ⟨f⟩, ⟨l⟩, ⟨s⟩, ⟨z⟩ are doubled.

🔊 Read this section aloudTap to start · tap again to stop

The convention

FLSZ CONVENTION After a single vowel grapheme, at the end of a single-morpheme content base:
f → ff  ·  l → ll  ·  s → ss  ·  z → zz

This is a positional, visual pattern — not a sound rule. The doubled grapheme belongs to the base and stays stable across the word family.

Content Words and Function Words

Word class shapes spelling. This is why ⟨grass⟩ ends in ⟨ss⟩ but ⟨his⟩ ends in ⟨s⟩.

🔊 Read this section aloudTap to start · tap again to stop

What kind of word is it?

📦 Content word

Carries meaning. Can anchor a word family — suffixes build related words from it.

grass → grassy
miss → missing
off → offset
🔗 Function word

Operates grammatically. Doesn't build suffix families. Carries a simpler, shorter spelling.

his · this · was
of · if · as
the · them · then
